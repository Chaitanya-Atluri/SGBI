ABOUT US

SGBI globally supports and manages such activities as poverty The Sustainable Global Business Initiative (SGBI) is a collaborative initiative between the Ann and Jack Graves Charitable Foundation and the Center for Global Business at The University of Texas at Dallas. The goal of the initiative is to promote economic advancement and poverty alleviation in developing countries, specifically focusing on African economies. SGBI supports and manages a wide range of activities, including developing sustainable business plans to empower people living in poverty, supporting academic conferences and workshops by networking and collaborating with African universities, evaluating the efficiency of economic development efforts in emerging economies, issuing an annual report and hosting recurring meetings with students and guest speakers. This initiative also provides scholarships for students and supports faculty conducting international sustainable poverty alleviation projects, with a particular focus on Africa.

Africa, as a target region of the SGBI, is a continent with immense natural and human resources as well as great cultural, ecological and economic diversity; however, it remains underdeveloped. The world’s poverty is increasingly concentrated within Africa. The United Nations Department of Economic and Social Affairs recently estimated that extreme poverty in the continent could rise further — by about 60 million people by 2030. SGBI understands that decelerating Africa’s deprivation is a global responsibility that comes with impediments. It requires more effort and teamworak among many stakeholders to improve the quality of life in Africa, which has become SGBI’s pressing mission.
